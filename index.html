House in ItabashiRenovation / Tokyo

During Japan’s economic bubble of the 1980s, condominium construction increased dramatically. Today, a 40-year-old condominium is often perceived as “old.” However, as the housing stock continues to grow, we must reconsider their value.

Rather than adopting a complete skeleton-and-infill approach that erases history, this renovation employs an upcycling strategy. By carefully assessing existing components and reinterpreting the "given," the project proposes a renewed architectural lifespan.
